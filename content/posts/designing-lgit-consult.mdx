## Starting Point

Every agency redesign starts with the same uncomfortable question: what does this site actually say about us? Our previous iteration was functional — clean components, solid architecture, the right framework choices — but it looked like every other tech consultancy. Rounded corners, blue accents, centered headings over card grids. The visual language said "SaaS startup," not "creative agency rooted in Leipzig's art and music scene."

We needed the site to feel like a publication. Something closer to 032c or Ssense Editorial than a B2B landing page.

## The Monochrome Decision

The most impactful choice was also the simplest: strip out all color. No blue accent. No gradient backgrounds. The palette became warm off-whites and near-blacks — `#f8f7f4` and `#111111` in light mode, inverted for dark. Color would come from photography and project work. The chrome stays monochrome.

This wasn't minimalism for its own sake. When you remove color as a crutch, typography has to carry the entire visual identity. That constraint forced better decisions everywhere else.

## Typography as Identity

We kept Geist — both Sans and Mono — but started using it like a design tool rather than a default. Display type at very light weights with tight tracking creates the editorial tension. Monospace at 11px with wide letter-spacing handles metadata, labels, and navigation. The contrast between a thin 80px headline and a small mono label underneath is where the personality lives.

The index numbering system — `001 — Services`, `002 — Work` — came directly from the glitch effect that was already drawing numbered indices on the canvas. We just pulled that system into the typography and made it coherent.

## Killing the Card

The old card component had `rounded-lg border shadow-sm` — a perfectly fine pattern that reads as "friendly startup." We replaced it with ruled lines. Sharp corners where borders exist at all. Content sits directly on the page with 1px dividers between items, like a magazine spread.

This applies everywhere: service listings, project grids, journal entries. Instead of hovering cards with shadows, items shift horizontally on hover with a subtle `translate-x-2`. The glitch system responds to the same interactions, so lines extend from elements as you move through the page.

## The Editorial Grid

The biggest layout change was breaking out of centered, symmetric compositions. Every section used to be: heading centered, paragraph centered, three-column card grid. Now the grid is 12 columns with intentional asymmetry — content might span columns 1-7 with a sidebar detail in 9-12. Heroes are left-aligned with massive vertical padding. Different sections use different column distributions.

The whitespace is deliberate. Fashion editorial layouts use space as a luxury signal. We doubled or tripled the padding between sections and let the typography breathe.

## Motion With Purpose

The original animations were uniform `fadeIn` with a 20px y-offset on every element. We replaced this with varied motion: text reveals line-by-line with stagger, some elements enter from the left or right, and scroll-linked parallax creates depth without overwhelming. Hover states connect to the glitch system — lines extend, elements shift, the canvas responds.

## What We Learned

Restraint is harder than addition. Every phase of this redesign involved removing something that felt safe — a shadow, a rounded corner, a color, a centered layout. What remains is more intentional and more distinctly us: a digital agency with analog sensibility, rooted in a city where warehouse galleries sit next to tech offices.

The site is the message. If we claim to bridge creative vision and technical execution, the website has to demonstrate that bridge — not just describe it.
