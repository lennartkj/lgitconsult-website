## The Premise

90 days. €60,000 in debt. Berlin doesn't wait.

Street Supply is a narrative-driven open-world game set across Berlin's neighborhoods — from Neukölln to Mitte, Kreuzberg to Friedrichshain. You drive, you deliver, you make choices that ripple through a cast of characters who all want something from you.

No moral compass. No karma meter. Just consequences arriving as newspaper headlines.

## What Makes It Different

**Real Berlin.** Built on Berlin's open 3D city data — 550,000 real buildings with actual roof geometry, real street layouts, real transit lines. A Kreuzberg Altbau looks like a Kreuzberg Altbau, not a generic box.

**Illustrated Interiors.** Inspired by GTA: Chinatown Wars — when you enter a building, the view shifts to hand-illustrated 2D interiors. Döner shops, recording studios, restaurants. Each one a story engine.

**The Clock Never Stops.** 90 in-game days. Each day is 6-7 minutes of real time. Interest accrues. People disappear. The math is tight — most first-time players won't clear the debt.

**No Cutscene Longer Than 90 Seconds.** Story arrives through phone calls, newspaper headlines, illustrated interior conversations, and the things that stop happening.

## The Pipeline

A hybrid pipeline combining OpenStreetMap, Berlin CityGML LoD2 data, GeoJSON open datasets, and SRTM elevation — generating a playable Berlin that's both technically authentic and artistically stylized.
