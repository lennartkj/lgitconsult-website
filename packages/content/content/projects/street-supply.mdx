You owe €60,000. You have 90 days. You're driving deliveries across Berlin to pay it off.

The game is set across Neukölln, Kreuzberg, Mitte, Friedrichshain — built from Berlin's open 3D city data. 550,000 real buildings with actual roof geometry, real street layouts, real transit lines. When you walk into a building, the perspective shifts to hand-illustrated 2D interiors — döner shops, recording studios, apartments.

Each in-game day runs about 6 minutes. Interest accrues. Characters come and go depending on what you do. There's no morality system — what happens happens, and you find out about it through phone calls and newspaper headlines.

The pipeline pulls from OpenStreetMap, Berlin CityGML LoD2, GeoJSON, and SRTM elevation data.
